# ◈ Quiet Minds — Calm Educational Games for Kids

### Problem

Most apps made for young children are designed to be addictive, not educational.
They flash, they beep, they reward every tap with fireworks and fanfare.
A four-year-old sitting with a tablet shouldn't feel like they're in a casino.

We wanted to build something different: a quiet place where a child can think.
One game. One question. One answer. No noise, no surprises, no pressure.

### Success Criteria

{% hint style="success" %}
A grandparent can hand a tablet to their grandchild, open this app, and walk away
knowing exactly what their child will encounter: simple, honest games that teach
real skills — counting, letters, shapes, colors, patterns — with nothing else
competing for the child's attention.

The app works for a 3-year-old who can't read and a 7-year-old who can.
It works without sound. It works in bright light and dim light.
It never asks for money, never pushes notifications, never shows ads.
{% endhint %}
